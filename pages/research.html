Multimodal Integration for Precision Medicine

Active Project
Timeline: 2024-Present
Collaborators: Gustave Roussy Cancer Institute

Project Overview

Multimodal integration for precision medicine leverages deep learning to combine diverse data types, such as genomics, histopathology, and clinical records, to provide personalized treatment insights. By using explainable methods, these models enhance interpretability, allowing clinicians to understand the underlying decision-making process, thus increasing trust and facilitating more informed clinical decisions.

Key Challenges

  • Data Heterogeneity: Integrating diverse data types, such as genomics, histopathology, and clinical records, which have different formats, scales, and noise levels.
  • Missing Modalities: Handling incomplete data where not all modalities are available for every patient, without compromising model performance.
  • Interpretability: Developing explainable models that maintain high accuracy while providing transparent and clinically relevant insights to support decision-making.
  • Generalization and Robustness: Ensuring that models generalize well across different patient populations and clinical settings, maintaining robustness to variations in data quality.

Impact

Multimodal integration using deep learning explainable methods holds the potential to improve precision medicine by providing more personalized treatment insights and supporting better-informed clinical decisions.

Multimodal Integration in Precision Medicine

Our research focuses on developing innovative approaches to integrate multiple data modalities in precision medicine. Through the CustOmics and Multimodal CustOmics frameworks, we work on combining various data types such as genomics, transcriptomics, and medical imaging to better understand disease mechanisms and improve treatment strategies.

Key Aspects:

  • Development of deep learning architectures for multi-omics integration
  • Integration of imaging and molecular data
  • Interpretable machine learning methods
  • Applications in precision oncology
×

Graph-based Methods for Biological Data

We develop graph-based approaches to analyze complex biological data, particularly focusing on spatial transcriptomics and histopathology analysis. Our Novae framework provides a sophisticated solution for understanding spatial organization in biological tissues.

Key Aspects:

  • Graph neural networks for spatial data analysis
  • Zero-shot domain inference
  • Batch effect correction
  • Spatial domain analysis
×

Deep Learning in Healthcare

Our research in deep learning for healthcare focuses on developing interpretable and robust solutions for medical applications. Through projects like Hyper-AdaC, we work on creating adaptive solutions that can handle the complexity of medical data while providing interpretable results.

Key Aspects:

  • Interpretable deep learning models
  • Adaptive clustering methods
  • Survival analysis
  • Clinical validation
